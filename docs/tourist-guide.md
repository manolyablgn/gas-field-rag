The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater located in the center of Rome, Italy. Construction began under Emperor Vespasian in AD 72 and was completed in AD 80 under his successor, Titus.

It could hold between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators, who watched gladiator contests, animal hunts, and public spectacles. The structure is made primarily of concrete and sand.

Visiting Hours: The Colosseum is open daily except December 25 and January 1. Tickets often include access to the nearby Roman Forum and Palatine Hill.

Tip: Visiting early in the morning or late afternoon helps avoid the largest crowds and the midday heat.
